CulinaryCut: A Physics-Aware Benchmark for Robotic Food Cutting in Deformable Simulation

Overview

Abstract

Existing robot manipulation benchmarks treat objects as rigid bodies, ignoring the deformation and topological changes fundamental to food preparation. We introduce CulinaryCut, a physics-aware benchmark for training and evaluating Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models on robotic food-cutting tasks.

Our simulation framework is built on MLS-MPM — a particle-based method that captures material deformation and fracture — coupled with a 7-DoF robot arm in the ManiSkill environment. The benchmark provides over 5,000 expert demonstrations across 7 food categories, paired with rich language annotations (≥5 per trajectory), synchronized end-effector force measurements, and multi-view RGB observations.

We evaluate three state-of-the-art VLA baselines — OpenVLA, Octo, and RDT-1B — and find systematic failures in physical reasoning and multi-step planning that rigid-object benchmarks fail to expose, motivating physics-aware VLA development.

Benchmark Design

Task Families

Two complementary task families expose distinct capability gaps in current VLA models.

Task Type 01

Spatial-Based Cutting

The robot interprets spatial language — “Cut from the left side,” “Slice at one-third from the top” — and executes a single downward knife pass at the grounded 3D position. Object pose is randomized to prevent positional overfitting.

Task Type 02

Division-Based Multi-Cut

The robot partitions food into N equal pieces through sequential passes. Each cut must account for prior deformation, requiring long-horizon geometric planning. All baselines show the largest performance drop here.

System

Simulation & Data Collection

  • Physics Engine — MLS-MPM. Moving Least Squares Material Point Method, a particle-based continuum solver that handles large deformations and topology changes. Material parameters (E, ν, ρ, σy) are set per food type from literature and swept with ±10% Gaussian noise.
  • Robot Environment — ManiSkill. A 7-DoF arm with a knife EEF runs in the GPU-accelerated ManiSkill simulator. Multi-view RGB observations from randomized camera configurations.
  • Data Collection. Expert demonstrations via human teleoperation and a motion planner, each annotated with ≥5 paraphrased language instructions and synchronized EEF force logs. 5,000+ base demos — 325K total trajectories.
  • Real-Robot Validation. Force curves from simulation are validated against a physical robot arm executing the same knife motion on real fruit, confirming physics-to-real consistency.
